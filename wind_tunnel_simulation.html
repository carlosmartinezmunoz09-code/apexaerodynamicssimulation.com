Cf (skin friction coefficient) = τwall / (½ρU²) — the dimensionless ratio of wall shear stress to freestream dynamic pressure. It measures how hard the airflow is dragging on the surface.

Cf is highest near the leading edge where the boundary layer is thinnest (steep velocity gradient → high wall shear). It decreases chord-wise as the boundary layer thickens, then spikes in the adverse pressure gradient zone (~55–80% chord) where decelerating flow strains the boundary layer toward separation. Riblets and piezo damping both reduce Cf — riblets by reorganising near-wall vortices, piezo by actively re-energising the boundary layer.
